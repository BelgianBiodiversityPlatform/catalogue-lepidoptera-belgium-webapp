Mesoligia literosa (Haworth, 1809)

A rare species in Belgium.

The larva lives on various species of Gramineae, cereal crops and sedges including Leymus arenarius, Ammophila arenaria and Dactylis glomerata. It feeds first on the roots, later inside a stem. Overwinters as a larva and pupates in a thin cocoon in leaf litter or in soil.

The adults fly from mid June till mid September in one generation. They come to light and sugar.

East-Germany, Mecklenburg-Vorpommern, Rügen, 25 July 2012.
