Tholera cespitis ([Denis & Schiffermüller], 1775)

A rare species throughout Belgium, more observed in the north of the country and in de Kempen.

The larva, when young feeds on the leaves of various species of Graminae like Deschampsia, Brachypodium and Nardus stricta, later on it consumes the stems near ground level. It feeds at night. Overwinters as an egg.

The adults fly in one generation a year from beginning of August till end September. They come to light and  less to sugar.

Belgium, Antwerpen, Oostmalle, 29 August 2004.
