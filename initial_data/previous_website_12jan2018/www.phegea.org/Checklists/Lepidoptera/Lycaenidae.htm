The Lycaenidae include around 6000 species most of which occur in the Tropics. The subfamily Riodininae is often given family rank. It occurs mainly in the Neotropics. The Lycaeninae have most representatives in the Holarctic region and the Polyommatinae are represented in all major biogeographical regions (Scoble 1992: 313).
Identification: Bink 1992, Ebert & Rennwald 1991, Emmet & Heath 1989, Forster & Wohlfahrt 1955, Hesselbarth, van Oorschot & Wagener 1995, Higgins & Hargreaves 1983, Higgins & Riley 1970 (+ later editions, reprints and traductions in several languages), Howarth 1973, Krzywicki 1959, Seitz 1906-1909, Whalley 1981, Wynhoff, Made & Swaay 1990.
