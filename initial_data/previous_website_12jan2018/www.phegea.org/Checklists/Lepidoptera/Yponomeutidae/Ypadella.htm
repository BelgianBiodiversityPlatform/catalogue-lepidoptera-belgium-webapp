Yponomeuta padella (Linnaeus, 1758)

A common species throughout Belgium.

The larvae live gregariously in a silken web on Prunus spinosa, Crataegus, and different species of Prunus.

The adults fly July and August. They come to light.

ID: identification of imago of following species as cagnagella, malinellus and padella are only with 100 % certainty done by reference to the genitalia. An aid to identify this species is the grey or whitish forewing with terminal cilia concolorous with adjacent part of wing, but this is a guide! In many cases the best guide to separate these species is often the foodplant!

Belgium, Antwerpen, Kontich, 26 July 2004.
