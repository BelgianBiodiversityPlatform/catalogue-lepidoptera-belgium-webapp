Triaxomera fulvimitrella (Sodoffsky, 1830)

A very rare species in Belgium, long time only known from one observation in May 1941 at Sourbrodt near Malmédy (LG). Next observations in 2008 in BR, 2010 in WV, 2014 in NA and 2015 again in LG. The species is becoming more common gradually.

The caterpillar lives in several species of mushrooms or fungi like Inonotus radiatus and Piptoporus betulinus with a preference for those growing on Fagus or Quercus. They are full-grown in April.

The adults have been observed mainly in May and June. They can be easily observed while resting on tree trunks and come to light. 

Belgium, Brabant, Rosières, 27 May 2008.
