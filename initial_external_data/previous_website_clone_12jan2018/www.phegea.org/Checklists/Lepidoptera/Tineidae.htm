Tineidae is a rather large, cosmopolitan family, in which about 3000 species are described. Unlike most of the other Lepidoptera larvae they do not consume live plant material, but feed on detritus, fungi, lichen or keratine (Scoble 1992: 226).
Identification: Brown 1953, Capuse 1968, Gaedike [1983], Gozmány 1965, Hannemann 1977, Passerin d'Entrèves 1975, Pelham-Clinton 1985, Petersen 1957-1958, 1960, 1961, 1969, [1983], Robinson 1979, 1986, Zagulyaev 1960, 1964, 1973, 1975, 1979, 1988, 1990a.
Nomenclature according to Fauna Europaea.
