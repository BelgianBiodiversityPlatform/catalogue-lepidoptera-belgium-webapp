Acleris hastiana (Linnaeus, 1758)

A rather common species throughout Belgium.

The larva spins several leaves together from which it feeds on the surrounding leaves. It lives on Salix, mainly on Salix repens and Salix aurita.

The adults of the first generation have been observed in June and July; the second generation starts to fly in August and occurs after hibernation till early April. A very variable species, almost a hundred different forms are described.

Belgium, Antwerpen, Willebroek, 20 March 2005.
