Lypusidae
Psychidae
Roeslerstammiidae

This family comprises about 600 species of which about 500 are found in the Old World. The larvae construct prominent larval silken cases in various shapes, covered with a variety of material, such as twigs, leaf debris, and sand grains (Scoble 1992: 229).
Identification: Ford 1946, Forster & Wohlfahrt 1960, Hättenschwiler 1985, Heylaerts 1884, Hoffmeyer 1974, Koch 1964, Kozhantshikov 1956, Sauter & Hättenschwiler 1991, Zagulyaev 1989h.
Nomenclature according to Fauna Europaea.
