Arctiidae
 

Arctiidae include about 11000 species of which about 6000 species occur in the Neotropics, though the family is represented in all major zoogeographical regions. The Lithosiinae, with about 2000 species, are mainly tropical. Ctenuchinae are often treated as a separate family. They also include about 2000 species of mainly tropical distribution. Arctiinae, again with around 2000 species, may not be monophyletic (Scoble 1992: 334).
Identification: de Freina & Witt 1987, Forster & Wohlfahrt 1960, Hoffmeyer 1974, Koch 1964, Kozhantshikov 1950, Razowski 1971, Skinner 1984.
